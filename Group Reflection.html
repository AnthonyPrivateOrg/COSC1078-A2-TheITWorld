Group Reflection

Group's Group Reflections

What went well?

After discussing with the group about what went well, the group thought that discussing the specifications of the assignment and assigning work at the beginning was something that went really well. As each member was assigned their own individual parts, everyone was responsible for their own sections, which helped everyone focus on their own parts. Another aspect that the team thought went well was setting deadlines. These deadlines helped each member stay on track, and in turn would help the team stay on track. These deadlines helped with having all the work done on time rather than leaving everything to the last minute. For the most part, the deadlines were met and everyone was on track for the majority of the time.

In terms of the meetings and what went well with them. The team believed that we were relatively consistent with the meetings, having a scheduled weekly online meeting in addition to the weekly tutorial we should all attend. And since we attended this meeting and most people were present, we never needed to redo meetings or hold a second meeting just to repeat the same content.

What could be improved?

The team has agreed that the overall quality of our work has not met our standard, and that for the next assignment we should all work towards provided quality work rather than a large quantity of work. We also think that discussing our ideal standard of work at the beginning of the assignment would have helped this, so that all members know what to aim for. With this assignment, we did not specify a standard early enough, resulting in some parts of the report/website to be lacking and did not meet our expectations

Another aspect that could be improved upon was deadlines and meeting deadlines. Regarding making deadlines, the team as unanimously agreed that making the deadlines was a good idea, but they should have been made earlier on. The deadlines we set were only set somewhere near the middle of the assignment rather than the start. This resulted in a slight rush at the end, when we had noticed that some of our worked needed improvement. By setting deadlines earlier we would have more time to review and would be less rushed in completed a high quality report/website. Regarding meeting deadlines, they were mostly met for the first half, however in the second half some of the members most likely overestimated themselves and started to relax too much, missing deadlines. As said before, at the beginning of the assignment, the majority of members were able to meet the deadlines, with some needing a bit more time to refine their sections. However, with the second half, more members were struggling to meet deadlines, this was both due to waiting for another member to finish their part or simply just leaving things to the last minute. Based on this, the members who missed the deadlines should strive to meet them during the next assignment or else there could be more consequences.

At least one thing that was surprising?

The group has agreed that something that was surprising was that our group was slightly ahead on most groups in our tutorial group. Our group had considered ourselves to be either on track or slightly behind. However, we were made aware, during our tutorial session, that our group had in fact done more work than some groups even though it was considered to be not a lot of work.

We were also surprised by the fact that our group was relatively good at communicating through MS Teams. We had initially struggle to find a reasonable time to schedule our meetings at the beginning. This was because we were trying to find times where all members would be present for at least half an hour. However, after the first few meetings, we were consistent with our meetings and were mostly on time, with most members present. The only reason for members missing out on a meeting was generally unexpected work commitments, which the team considered to be acceptable.

One of the most surprising aspects of the team was the inconsistency in work effort. As said before, initially most members were meeting the set deadlines, both completing the work and posting it in MS Teams. However, after the first deadline, deadlines started to not be met and some of the work submitted was also not up to standard. This was surprising, considering the first deadline was met considerable well, but was suddenly turned around, and the second deadline was not met in the same manner.

At least one thing that you have learned about groups?

In terms of what our team learned about groups, we had learned that it is very difficult to manage teams and times. Everyone had their own commitments, in terms of both work and other courses they were taking. In this sense each individual had a very difficult time managing their own time, and as a whole group we struggled for all members to meet the set deadlines on time. We also learned that, as a group, managing each member’s priorities was very difficult especially when two members worked on the same section.

We had also learned that setting deadlines are very important when working in groups/teams. Setting deadlines would help keep members on track and hold them accountable for each of their assigned sections. During this assignment we saw that some members struggled to meet the set deadlines, this could be for multiple reasons, but the deadlines hold them accountable for not completing their sections on time and would indicate to the team who is holding the team back or who needs help.

Does our Github log reflect our group work?

Our group believes that in terms of the number of commits, the GitHub log should show an accurate representation on how the work was distributed, how even it was distributed, and how often each member used GitHub. In terms of number of lines committed, the GitHub logs would be more skewed towards Victor, as the group had decided to use his template from assignment 1, and therefore he would have submitted more lines of code for generating the base template. As such, our group believes that the GitHub logs may not be entirely accurate, however, if there are logs for MS Teams, then it would reflect our group work better. The reason for this is because, GitHub lines committed doesn’t includes lines in Word Documents, which is why we believe that number of commits would reflect our group work better than lines committed.



Individual Group Reflections

Anthony's Reflection

I believe that overall our group had worked decently well, of course with any group, there will be certain aspects that need to be improved upon, but there are also aspects that worked well despite that. One of the aspects that I think we did well on was assigning work early on in the assignment. As a group, we had gone through the assignment specifications in our first meeting and distributed the work roughly evenly. Another aspect that I think went reasonably well, was that our group had set deadlines for the group. Assigning deadlines helped with keeping members on track and ensuring that they are held accountable for their own sections. The final aspect that I thin went well in the group was that our group was relatively good at joining the online meeting consistently. Group work is especially difficult if members don’t communicate between each other, and this group seems to communicate rather well.

In my opinion, although overall most members met deadlines on time, there were a few who didn’t or didn’t show evidence that they did through posting their work to either GitHub or MS Teams. This is something I consider to be especially important in group work, especially if one person relies on another’s part. The quality of our work should also be improved and we should have set a standard for members to aim for. Some of our work did not meet our standard and we had only noticed this later on in the assignment which is not ideal.

Something that surprised me was that even though there were two means of posting work the group did not utilise these nearly as much as they should have. By posting our work in our shared drives, the group can see evidence of each members work effort and also to show that members are actually meeting deadlines rather than simply saying they’ve completed the work. Something I had learned about groups is that it is sometimes extremely difficult to find a time for all members to be consistently present. Although our group had found a time where members were generally available, there were a few meetings where we had missing members for both the face-to-face meetings and the online meetings.

Personally, I think that in terms of number of commits, the GitHub log reflects our groups work, in terms of who was assigned which section. However, in terms of lines committed, they would be skewed towards one member, Victor, as he was the one making the website templates for the group. The reason for this was that we were using his template and it would be easier for him to upload it rather than having everyone recreate it.

Artem's Reflection

I believe we did quite well as a group. There are certainly things that need to be improved, but I don’t think there could ever be a group that wouldn’t need any sort of improvement. The aspect, that I reckon, we went really well at was establishing a timeline of deadlines and distributing the work.

Meeting said deadlines is probably one of the things we could improve, however, we set them up with room for error and time for polishing up our work, so I reckon we stayed on track relatively well. Another thing that could be improved is establishing a common format of our work, so that we wouldn’t have to change things around in the end to keep the work consistent.

At the moment, it’s hard to properly collaborate as, a lot of the times, our timetables don’t match up. So splitting up the work evenly, working on it independently, asking for help and assisting each other, in my opinion, was the right way to go. A timeline helped us stay on track and allowed us, to my surprise, to not fall too much behind. Personally, being just out of high school, I found that my teachers pushing me to do work actually helped me. Now that no one actually cares whether I do my work or not and I have to organise myself on my own, deadlines really did help a lot. I will definitely take note of that for my future projects.

I’ve also learnt that, in groups, a leadership role is important. In our group that role was fulfilled by Anthony and I am very thankful to him for that. He made sure we are staying on track, provided help where we needed and kept the workflow moving. He motivated and pushed me, which I believe is another big reason as to why we never fell back too far behind.

What I have learned from groups was that it was hard to manage/make sure that people had done their parts to a satisfactory degree and that wasted valuable time trying to catch up with those things.
