Artefacts

For one of our presentable artefacts, we had decided to develop a mobile app prototype. This prototype will demonstrate the basic flow of the app if it were to be fully developed and used by a real world user. Users would start by logging in or registering. They can then view their current bookings or make a booking. Once they go through the process of booking a parking slot, their current bookings list will no longer be empty. They can then cancel their previously booked slot or notify the system that they have arrived. The limit of our prototype is that users cannot make multiple bookings and input their own details as we use pre-set values. As such, the prototype will only demonstrate the basic flow of a user making a booking and either cancelling it or notifying the system of their arrival.

A physical miniature was made to simulate the electronic side of the parking system as one of the artefacts. It shows three different situations, one being an occupied parking spot/booked parking spot, another being an empty parking spot and finally one that is a parking spot "in progress" so to speak where the demonstration will happen. As a car moves into the parking spot, once it reaches the sensor, the light will turn red as it is occupied and will also trigger the bollard behind it to move up. Once a car wants to leave, as it is reversing from the sensor, the bollard will come down and the overhead light will turn green.
