IT Work

Overview

To have a ‘real insight’ into the IT industry, we decided to interview an IT professional who is a technical lead at a property, technology start-up called Landchecker. From the questions provided below, both the ones recommended with the addition of the questions we believed were interesting and insightful, we were able to see what it is like to work in this industry on a day-to-day basis, as well as how to prosper and make a successful career out of your passion.

  2. Please tell us about the industry you work in.
  3. What other kinds of work do you have to do?

  5. Who are all the different people you interact with in your work? Please tell us about them.

Notes From Interview

  1. Please tell us about your IT work. What exactly do you do?
    • Technical Lead for a property tech start-up Landchecker
    • Lead solution design for the features that we developed on a day-to-day basis
    • 60% hands on development
    • 40% management
  2. Please tell us about the industry you work in.
    • We work in a very niche tech start-up focused on the property industry. Our company, Landchecker provides an all-in-one source of property information together with data analysis tools to help professionals and homeowners make informed decisions, faster.
  3. What other kinds of work do you have to do?
    • Aside from daily start-ups, my work involves understanding new technologies that we can adapt into our platform.
    • This involves talking to 3rd party integrators that provide niche services.
  4. Could you go through what a typical day looks like for you?
    • We do daily stand-ups at 9 AM either onsite or remotely via slack. Our stand-ups include we do on a day-to-day basis if we have issues or blockers.
    • I usually par with some of the developers who needs help, I do most code reviews and their PR Pull Request.
    • I also manage deployments and ensure that everything is working post-development.
  5. Who are all the different people you interact with in your work? Please tell us about them.
    • Since I lead a team, I have to interact / engage with the software developers regarding their current activities.
    • And I also inform other stakeholders within the organisation such as Operations, Sales, Business Development and Support
  6. Please tell us about your interactions with other IT professionals.
    • Most of my interactions are within my development team. This involves mentoring, pairing with the developers, understanding their strengths and weaknesses, the challenges that they are going through.
  7. What about your interactions with clients or investors?
    • We do monthly share holder meetings for our investors. My job includes preparing a technical report on the progress of the engineering activities.
  8. What aspects of your work do you spend most time on? Please tell us about these.
    • I’m a hands-on technical lead, so while I manage people, I also develop other features for our platform. I will consider this around 60%, so I still do platform-development.
    • The other 40% involves solution design and management.
  9. Which aspects of your work do you find most challenging and how have do you overcome these adversities?
    • When you are solving complex problems and issues, solution design is challenging because you will need to iterate through your hypothesis and collaborate with your team members to get it right, implement it, and see if it really works in the end.
  10. Can you share an example of the work you do that best captures the essence of the IT industry?
    • I will share with you the evolution of our platform that we built in Landchecker
  11. Was this your first choice for a career? If so, was this your first IT profession?
    • I studied surveying/engineering, but I always wanted to work with computers and software development, so I learned on the job.
    • My first job was a junior web developer.
  12. What would be the best advice you would give to someone trying to get into the IT industry?
    • You need to be persistent and be solution oriented. You cannot literally give up, especially on complex scenarios.
